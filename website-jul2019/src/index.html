Caternary Forces Simulation

UBC Mars Colony Design Team

One of the obstacles we faced with the construction of our life-sized expandable airlock was designing and constructing it to withstand a Martian environment on Earth. In particular, the frame and mylar covering must be flexible to withstand repeated pressurization and depressurization, rigid against severe Martian climates, but strong enough to support the weight of four astronauts and their equipment.



My project involved simulating the forces on the rope bindings of the airlock, knowing that gravity and atmospheric pressure are significant factors on Mars.
